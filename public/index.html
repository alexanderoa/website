About

Alexander Asemota

Statistician

Hi, and welcome to my website! I’m a PhD candidate in the statistics department at UC Berkeley. My work broadly relates to fairness, transparency, explainability, and auditing of computational systems. Within this realm, I’ve worked on improving counterfactual explanations for ML models, developed methods for inference on fairness, and proposed and approach to rigorously assess software for DNA forensics. As a statistician, I am drawn to flexible methods that require few assumptions, particularly those that leverage machine learning models. Additionally, I’m interested in experimental design and causal inference, and I often apply concepts from these domains to the problems that I work on.
